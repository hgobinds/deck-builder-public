## Slide 3: The Wrong Question

Message: "Will AI replace consultants?" is the wrong question. It creates fear and misses the point.
Audience job: Shift perspective. Recognise the fear and let it go.
Content:
- The fear: AI takes consultant jobs
- Why it's wrong: the highest-value parts of consulting are not automatable — reading a client, diagnosing a real problem, building trust, making judgment calls
- The right question: which parts of consultant work should AI handle, and which should stay human?
Visual: Split or contrast layout. "Wrong question" crossed out or faded. "Right question" highlighted.
Speaker note: "I've heard this concern. It's worth addressing directly. The consultants at risk are not those who use AI — they're the ones who keep spending their time on the parts AI can already do better."
Dependencies: None

## Slide 4: Two Layers of Consulting Work

Message: Consulting work has two distinct layers — the operating layer and the judgment layer. AI belongs in one of them.
Audience job: Understand the distinction. See where AI fits without feeling threatened.
Content:
Operating layer (AI handles):
- Setting up workspaces
- Logging meeting notes
- Formatting deliverables
- Routing outputs
- Drafting status updates

Judgment layer (consultant keeps):
- Diagnosing the real problem
- Reading client dynamics
- Making recommendations
- Building trust
- Deciding what matters

Visual: Two-column layout or layered visual. Clean separation. Judgment layer should feel elevated.
Speaker note: "The goal of our AI integration plan is not to automate consulting. It's to automate the operating layer so you have more capacity for the judgment layer — which is where the actual value is."
Dependencies: Source: Consultant Tooling Proposal (internal)

## Slide 5: Where AI Fits in Your Workflow

Message: AI has a specific role at every stage of a consultant's work — not just at one point.
Audience job: See AI as woven into the workflow, not bolted on.
Content: The 5-stage consultant operating loop with one AI assist per stage:
- Intake + Discovery → AI turns messy client input into a structured brief
- Scope + Proposal → AI drafts scope, identifies risks, flags missing information
- Delivery → AI routes deliverables, formats outputs, checks against acceptance criteria
- Review + Client Comms → AI drafts client updates, flags scope changes, logs decisions
- Handoff → AI builds the handoff package so clients can use what you built

Visual: Horizontal flow diagram or loop. Each stage labelled. AI assist shown as a supporting layer beneath each step — not replacing the stage, supporting it.
Speaker note: "You still run every stage. AI handles the parts that are repetitive, forgettable, or time-consuming — so you stay focused on the work that requires you."
Dependencies: Source: Consultant Tooling Proposal (internal) — operating loop

## Slide 6: What the Tools Look Like

Message: These are not abstract concepts — here are four tools you could use today.
Audience job: Make it concrete. See what this looks like in practice.
Content: Four tools with one-line descriptions:

1. **Intake Brief Builder** — Turns a messy client email or call transcript into a structured brief: stated problem, assumptions, risks, and missing information.
2. **Meeting Notes Processor** — Turns a transcript or rough notes into decisions, action items, risks, and next steps. Saves in the right place automatically.
3. **Review Gate** — Checks a deliverable before it leaves your desk: scope match, unsupported claims, formatting, next steps included.
4. **Handoff Package Builder** — Creates documentation so the client can actually use what you built — not just "send the files and hope."

Visual: 2x2 grid or simple list with icons. Each tool gets a name, one-line job, and one-line outcome.
Speaker note: "Each tool does one job. You don't need to learn a new system — you use the right tool at the right moment in the workflow you already follow."
Dependencies: Source: Consultant Tooling Proposal (internal) — tool descriptions
