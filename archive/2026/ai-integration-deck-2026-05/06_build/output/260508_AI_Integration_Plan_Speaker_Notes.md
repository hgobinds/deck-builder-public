## Slide 2 — AI Is Already Here

AI is already in consulting work. Other firms are using it in delivery right now — structuring client outputs, drafting communications, processing research. So I'm not going to spend time on "AI is coming." The question I want to answer today is what this should look like for our organization.

---

## Slide 3 — The Wrong Question

The concern I hear most is: does this mean we get replaced? Worth saying out loud, because if it's in the back of your head it makes it hard to think about anything else. My honest answer is no. The parts of this job clients actually pay for — figuring out what the real problem is, reading the room, building the kind of trust that makes them call you back — none of that is automatable. What AI is genuinely good at is the layer underneath all that. And that's what this plan addresses.

---

## Slide 4 — Two Layers

Think about a typical engagement week. There's the work you're there to do: the diagnosis, the client conversation, the judgment call on what to recommend. And then there's everything around it. Notes from Tuesday's call. The folder structure. Formatting the deck. Making sure version 4 went to the right person. That second bucket is real work and it takes real time, but it's not where the value is. The plan is to get AI handling that so you have more room for the first bucket.

---

## Slide 5 — Where AI Fits

Intake: AI turns whatever the client sent into a structured brief. Scope: it flags gaps and unanswered questions. Delivery: it routes files and tracks what went where. Client comms: it drafts the status update, logs the decision from Tuesday's call. Handoff: it builds the package so the client can actually use what we built. You run every stage. AI handles the parts that take time but don't need your judgment.

---

## Slide 6 — What the Tools Look Like

Four tools, each does one thing. The Intake Brief Builder structures whatever the client sent — stated problem, actual problem hypothesis, what we know, what we're assuming, what we still need to ask. The Meeting Notes Processor takes a transcript and gives you decisions, actions, and risks, already saved in the right place. The Review Gate checks a deliverable before it leaves your desk. The Handoff Package Builder creates documentation clients can actually open without calling you three weeks later asking what something means. These aren't mockups. We're building them.

## Slide 8 — The Plan

Three phases. Phase 1: core delivery loop, seven tools, intake through handoff. That's what the sample implementation trial is testing. Phase 2: control layer — change tracking, workspace auditing, decision logging. Phase 3: practice growth tools. We prove Phase 1 works on real engagements first, then we build out.
