# Stage 03: Narrative

## What This Stage Does

Turn the brief and approved sources into the argument the deck needs to make.

## What To Load

| Situation | Load | Skip These | Output | Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Default | `../01_intake/output/deck-brief.md`, `../02_source-discovery/output/source-brief.md`, project `project-state.md` | Raw saved pages unless checking a claim, build drafts | `output/narrative-arc.md` | Source discovery is approved or skipped |

## Process

1. **Discuss first.** Before writing, share your proposed thesis and structure with the user:
   - What is the core argument you plan to make?
   - What tension or problem will anchor the deck?
   - Are there objections or angles the user wants included or avoided?
2. Get agreement on the direction before drafting.
3. Define the core thesis.
4. Describe the audience starting point.
5. Identify the tension, problem, or change.
6. Sequence evidence and implications.
7. Name objections to handle.
8. State the recommended decision or action.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Core thesis, audience starting point, tension, evidence sequence, objections, and ask are clear.
- Claims are supported by the approved source brief or marked as user-provided.
- The narrative is strong enough to storyboard without returning to raw notes.

## Approval Gate

Review required by: deck owner.
Approved to proceed?: yes/no.

## What Not To Do

- Do not write the narrative arc without first aligning on the thesis direction.
- Do not create a slide list before the argument is clear.
- Do not introduce unsupported claims.
- Do not turn the source brief into a research report.
